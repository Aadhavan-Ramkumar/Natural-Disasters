Natural Disasters




Earthquakes

An earthquake is the shaking in the Earth's surface caused by the movement of tectonic plates. Most earthquakes can't even be felt, while some earthquakes can destroy whole communities or even cities!

Volcanoes

A volcano is a hole in the Earth's crust that spews lava, gas, and ash. It can take many years for the land affected by the eruption to recover, sometimes even thousands of years!

Tsunamis

A tsunami is a large wave caused by earthquakes or volcanoes underwater. Most tsunamis can go as high as 30 m (100 ft) and reach speeds of up to 700 kmph (500 mph), as fast as a jet airplane!

Hurricanes

A hurricane is a large, swirling storm formed in the sea. They can reach speeds between 119 kmph (74 mph) to more than 252 kmph (157 mph)! The strongest ones can uproot whole trees and destroy whole houses! They can last for a week or more.

Tornadoes

A tornado is a violent rotating column of air that can reach speeds of up to 500 kmph (300 mph). They usually last for a mere few seconds to an hour.

Droughts

A drought is a period of time where little to no rain falls, making the ground very dry. They can last for weeks, months, or even years! Regions like the Southwest US are places where droughts are more frequent.

Blizzards

A blizzard is a long-lasting snowstorm with fast winds and heavy snowfall. During this period, the temperature can get well below the freezing point. Icy roads and sleet are also common.

Wildfires

A wildfire is an uncontrollable forest fire generating in combustible vegetation. These are more common in arid deserts like Australia and the Southwest US. They can last for many days, or even a month.
